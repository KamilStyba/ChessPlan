02 — OPENING LAB

Your Repertoire: Queen's Gambit & Sicilian

Wednesday's 30′ lab alternates: odd weeks White, even weeks Black. Max 8 moves deep — at 1100 you win with ideas and plans, not memorized theory.

♕White: Queen's Gambit

  • Core plans: pressure d5, develop Nc3–Nf3–Bg5/Bf4–e3, castle short, rooks to c1/d1.
  • Learn the Carlsbad structure (after cxd5 exd5): the minority attack b4–b5 is your first "real plan" — and Euwe's book explains exactly this kind of thinking.
  • vs QGA (2…dxc4): don't panic — e3/e4, Bxc4, you regain the pawn; watch out for …b5 tricks.
  • vs Slav (2…c6): simple development, e3, consider Qb3 pressure on b7/d5.
Lab rotation: W1 Exchange QGD + minority attack · W2 vs QGA · W3 vs Slav · W4 traps & sidelines (Albin, Englund) + 3 model games.
Lichess explorer ↗ chess.com explorer ↗ Chessable course ↗

♛Black: Sicilian Defense

  • Pick ONE line vs the Open Sicilian and marry it. With Aagaard on your shelf, an aggressive setup suits you — but consistency matters more than the choice.
  • Reality check: at your level ~70% of games are Anti-Sicilians. Your priority is meeting 2.c3 (Alapin), the Smith–Morra and the Grand Prix.
  • Core plans: fight for d4, queenside play with …a6/…b5, rook to c8, hit e4; the thematic …Rxc3 exchange sac is pure Aagaard material.
  • Don't fear early queen raids and Scholar's-mate tries — develop with tempo, punish them.
Lab rotation: W1 your main line vs Open · W2 vs Alapin 2.c3 · W3 vs Smith–Morra & Grand Prix · W4 model games in your line.

⚠Repertoire Gap: Black vs 1.d4

  • You told me what you play as White and vs 1.e4 — but what about 1.d4?
  • Coach's pick: the QGD as Black. You already study the Queen's Gambit as White — playing both sides doubles your understanding of every plan, including defending against your own minority attack.
  • Solid alternative: the Slav. Decide by week 3 and add it to the lab rotation.
Action: one extra 15′ explorer session this week — play 5 casual games with the QGD as Black and see how it feels.

↗ TRACK IT

Progress Metrics

Rating alone lies. Track these monthly to see where the gains are really coming from.

📊What to measure

  • Elo trend — screenshot your rating graph on the 1st of every month.
  • Tactics accuracy % — aim for >80% on puzzles matched to your level (Chess.com puzzle rating ≈ game rating).
  • Blunders per game — engine analysis of Saturday's classical. Target: below 1 critical error/game by week 8.
  • Endgame conversion — log won / drawn / lost in your journal when you reach a theoretically won ending. Target: 80% converted by phase 2.
  • Opening depth — how many moves before you're "out of book"? Should grow a move or two each month.

🗓Review rhythm

  • Weekly (Sunday): already built in — name your "mistake of the week" and log it.
  • Month-end: compare Elo vs. 4 weeks ago. Run a 60-min tactics test (20 puzzles, full calculation — no peeking). Note improvement or stagnation.
  • Phase end (week 4 / 8 / 12): reread the blunder journal. Still making the same mistake? That pattern becomes the following week's Monday motif.
05 — THE DISCIPLINE

Grandmaster's 10 Rules

  1. Before every move: Checks, Captures, Threats — yours and your opponent's.
  2. No bullet. 15+10 or slower only (Sunday fun is the exception).
  3. Solve puzzles like real games — calculate to the end before touching a piece.
  4. Analyse every game you play. Brain first, engine second.
  5. Keep a blunder journal; reread it before each play session.
  1. Openings = your two weapons only. Every game a QG or a Sicilian — repetition builds mastery.
  2. Ahead? Trade pieces. Behind? Complicate (the Aagaard way).
  3. Consistency beats heroics — tired? Take the rest day guilt-free.
  4. After a tough loss or frustration: walk away for 10 minutes before the next game. Tilt erases training gains faster than anything.
  5. Move your body 2–3× a week — 20-min walk, jog, stretch. Physical stamina directly supports focus in long classical games.

🇵🇱PZSzach Corner

  • The target: II kategoria norm — 1800 (M) / 1600 (W) performance in one classified tournament; one fulfillment is enough.
  • Score at least 1/3 of the points, classical tempo required.
  • Your category is lifetime — play boldly.
